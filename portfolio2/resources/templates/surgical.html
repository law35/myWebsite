    Surgical Technology: American College of Health care:

    Anatomy & Physiology I:

    • Study levels of organization of the body, simplest to complex.
    • Negative and positive feedback mechanisms.
    • Anatomic positions and terminology of different body regions.
    • Body cavities, their membranes and organs in each cavity.
    • Organic molecules of cell membranes, their functions
    • and the functions of cell organelles and cell transport systems.
    • Cell metabolism growth and development.
    • (Transcription & Translation of DNA) protein synthesis;
    • Characteristics and four major categories of tissues;
    • Exocrine and Endocrine systems; Musculoskeletal system;
    • cellular mechanisms of muscle contraction,
    • Bone tissue growth and homeostasis.

  • .II

    Anatomy & Physiology II:

    • Examine general structure and functions of the digestive system
    • and the mechanisms of chemical and mechanical digestion.
    • Divisions of the nervous system both structure and fucntion
    • and transmissions of impulses within neurological systems.
    • The different regions of the brain.
    • Division of the nervous system along with sensory pathways.
    • Anatomy and physiology of the eye and ear.
    • Subdivision of the circulatory system(Lymphatic system);
    • lymph production / function.
    • Composition and function of blood plasma,
    • primary hemopoietic tissue and blood cell synthesis;
    • Chemical stages of clotting.

  • .III

    Anatomy & Physiology III:

    • Study the pericardial and endocardium of the heart.
    • The chambers of the heart along with its valves and vessels.
    • Cardiac cycles, coronary circulation, output and Starling's law.
    • Neuro-regulation of heart rate and force of contraction;
    • Structure and function of major arteries, viens, capillaries,
    • arterial and venous anastomoses.
    • Pathways of hepatic portal circulation;
    • modifications of fetal circulation and blood pressure regulation;
    • Autonomic regulation of blood vessel diameter.

    Intro to Surgical Technology I:

    • Delinate the development of the Surgical Technology field.
    • Identify the members of the surgical team and their roles.
    • Understand the facets of a surgical technologist job description.
    • Demomstrate the principles of communication within the O.R.
    • Summarize the types of health care facilities.
    • Distinguish between sterile and non-sterile team members.
    • Identify the indicators of sterility.

    Intro to Surgical Technology II:

    • An introduction to surgical instrumentation
    • along with specific nomenclature (names)and classification.
    • Concepts of sterilization and disinfection;
    • and their effects on wound healing.
    • The care, use and handling of instrumentation and suture.
    • Detailed study and application of common surgical procedures.

    • don sterile personal protection equipment.
